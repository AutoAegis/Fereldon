The Law

Laws of the Empire

Assault

The act of intentionally causing another person to fear imminent physical or verbal harm.

Battery

The act of intentionally making unwanted or harmful physical contact with another person.

Homicide

The act of unlawfully causing the death of another human being, whether intentionally or through reckless or negligent behaviour.

Erotic Assault

The act of engaging in public erotic behaviour or contact through force, coercion, or manipulation.

Theft (Larceny)

The act of taking someone else's property without permission and with the intent to permanently deprive them of it.

Burglary

The act of unlawfully entering a building or structure with the intent to commit a crime inside, usually theft.

Vandalism

The act of intentionally damaging, destroying, or deforming property that does not belong to you.

Disorderly Conduct

The act of behaving in a disruptive, dangerous, or socially inappropriate manner than disturbs public peace.

Illegal Ownership

The act of possessing, carrying, smuggling, or using items that does not belong to you.

Fraud

The act of intentionally deceiving another person or entity to obtain money, property, or an unfair advantage.

Embezzlement

The act of unlawfully taking money or property that has been entrusted to you, typically within a position of employment or authority.

Money Laundering

The act of conceling the origins of illegally obtained money by passing it through legitimate businesses or financial transactions.

Identity Theft

The act of using another person's personal information, such as their name or financial details, without authorisation to commit fraud or gain benefits.
