The Law

Laws of the Empire

Political Crimes

Offences that threaten the stability, authority, or operations of the Empire.

Treason

The act of betraying the Empire through aiding enemies, espionage, or plotting against the sovereign authority.

Sedition

The act of encouraging rebellion, disorder, or resistance against lawful imperial authority.

Corruption

The act of abusing political power for personal gain, including bribery, favoritism, and misuse of office.

Social Crimes

Offences that harm individuals, disrupt peace, or endanger the public.

Assault

The act of intentionally causing another person to fear imminent physical or verbal harm.

Battery

The act of intentionally making unwanted or harmful physical contact with another person.

Homicide

The unlawful act of causing the death of another being through intent, recklessness, or negligence.

Erotic Assault

The act of engaging in erotic behaviour or contact through force, coercion, manipulation, or public indecency.

Vandalism

The intentional destruction, defacement, or damage of property belonging to another.

Disorderly Conduct

Behaviour that disrupts public peace, including dangerous, unruly, or inappropriate actions.

Illegal Ownership

The unlawful possession, carrying, or acquisition of restricted, stolen, or prohibited items.

Economic Crimes

Offences involving financial deception, theft, or abuse of resources.

Theft (Larceny)

The act of taking property without the owner's consent with intent to permanently deprive them of it.

Burglary

Unlawfully entering a structure with intent to commit a crime, typically theft.

Fraud

Deception used to obtain money, property, or unlawful advantage.

Embezzlement

The theft of funds or property entrusted to a person in a position of authority or employment.

Money Laundering

The concealment of illegal funds by processing them through legitimate channels.

Identity Theft

The use of another person’s personal information without permission to commit fraud or gain benefits.
